---
title: About RFCs
---

An RFC is a *request for comment*. The CatColab team uses RFCs as a way to
communicate with each other and the community about features we intend to build
and the mathematics those features are based on.

A distinctive feature of RFCs is that they represent a design at a particular
point in time, as understood by a particular person. Thus, an RFC should have a
date and an author. Unlike for other kinds of documentation, there is no
expectation that an RFC will be updated over time as its implementation evolves.

At this time, the format of an CatColab RFC is not standardized. In the RFCs
written so far, we've taken inspiriation from the [RFC
template](https://github.com/rust-lang/rfcs/blob/master/0000-template.md) used
by the [Rust](https://rust-lang.org/) language.
